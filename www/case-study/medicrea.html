MEDICREA

Medicrea is a ground breaking medical company that specialises in customsed spinal surgery. They sell spinal rods customised to each patient that needs spinal surgery. I was brought on because they want to create a single full-service platform that helps surgeons plan their surgeries, order products, and conduct post-operative analysis.

Role

UX, UI, Branding, Copy, QA

Product Manager

Michael Texeira

1

Research Phase

Our main two groups of users are the surgeons and the Medicrea lab team. To gain an understanding of each user, we conducted multiple user interviews and contextual inquiries, and gathered data about surgeons. Then based on this research, I created personas and then mapped out the primary user flows.

“The surgeon is the impetus for the order, driving the initial strategy for the surgery and approving the final order for the spinal rod. Also at any point, he/she can cancel the case.”

USER INTERVIEW WITH
 CHIEF VP OF PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT

Primary User: The Surgeon

Gene is a senior spinal surgeon at White Plains Hospital, whose primary goal is patient and personal satisfaction in his surgeries. He’s interested in the cutting edge technology that Medicrea offers for his patient, but as he's busy, he doesn't have a lot of time to learn unintuitive software or manually enter repetitive data when he’s planning his surgery strategy and ordering his product.

User Needs

  • To quickly see his most urgent tasks
  • To easily input initial surgery plan and pre-op photos in a new case
  • To give feedback and/or approve a recommendation
  • To visit a single platform for all communication

“As the surgeon is busy, the lab team essentially acts as a project manager, tracking down info and moving the case along. We also translate the surgeon's surgery strategy into the customised product.”

USER INTERVIEW WITH
US PRODUCT SPECIALIST

Secondary User: The Lab Product Specialist

Daniela Jones is a product specialist on the Medicrea US Lab team who handles US orders. She is responsible for liaising with the surgeon and ensuring accurate and timely construction of the customised spinal rod. However most time-intensive part of her job is following up with the medical staff on filling out the required fields. Because the software is not intuitive, workarounds are necessary.

User Needs

  • To quickly see her most urgent tasks
  • To automatically pull medical information from various touchpoints
  • To easily create, share, and edit surgery recommendations
  • To visit a single platform for all communication

3

Design Phase

Next, the new branding and user-centric strategy had to be applied to every page, which mean this next phase took the longest. On each page, I designed for each user, refining all actions to allow them to successfully meet their goals.

Good Morning! Here are your To-Dos today

The Dashboard shows your most urgent tasks today and a preview of what’s ahead. All the ways a surgeon or lab member would want to slice or dice their list of open cases, the Dashboard has a shortcut for it.

FINDING ONE CASE OUT OF HUNDREDS

The goal of Cases is for users to find the specific case memo they needed. I focus on users filtering by status or timing, sorting the results, and viewing the results either as cards or a list.

FORMS, FORMS, FORMS

A large part of this platform are form fields. Most designers don’t find this sexy… but I do in a nerdy way. How can I make input fields cute and easy
to use?

THE HARDEST WORKING PAGE

The Case Memo needs to show the case’s current status, past activity, next step, surgeon’s brief, lab’s proposed plan, post-operative analysis, and allow feedback between the surgeon & lab specialist.

4

Development Phase

During the design phase, I worked very closely with the development team in France, using Slack for communication, InVision for prototyping, and Zeplin for asset hand off, to make sure everything was seamless for them. Now, during the development phase, I’m QAing as the developers are finishing. Stay tuned for the launch in the fall!
